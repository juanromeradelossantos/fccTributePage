Santiago Ramón y Cajal

Santiago Ramón y Cajal was a Spanish scientist and doctor, considered father of the neuroscience. He was the first person of Spanish origin to win a Noble Prize in Medicine and he shared it with Camillo Golgi recognising their work in the nervous system structure.

Timeline

  • Born on 1 May 1952, Navarre, Spain.
  • Graduated in 1873 in medical studies by the University of Zaragoza.
  • Served in the Spanish army as medical officer.
  • Received his doctorate in medicine in 1877.
  • In 1879 he became the director of the Zaragoza Museum.
  • Married Silveria Fañanás García.
  • In 1883 he became anatomy professor in the University of Valencia.
  • He learned about Golgi's method in 1887, a cell staining method that he improved, allowing him to turn his attention to the central nervous system.
  • In 1892 became professor in Madrid.
  • In 1906 he was awarded with the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine.
  • In 1922 founded the Laboratory of Biological Investigations, later renamed Cajal Institute.
  • On October 17, 1934 he died in Madrid at the age of 82.
